Introduction


Maharashtra is the second most populous and third largest state of India. It spread over 307713 Sq. Km and situated over west coast Of India Maharashtra occupies the western and central part of the country and as per statistical data available, Maharashtra has long Coastline stretching 720 kilometers along the Arabian Sea, which is divided,among six districts as under palghar district 110.50,Thane 16.50 Mumbai City & Mumbai Suburban 114.00,Raigad 121.00 Ratnagiri 238.00 ,Sindhudurg 120.00 Total 720.00. out of this 720 Kms Coastal length 400 Kms length is naturally protected, & About 320 Kms length is vulnerable to erosion.out of this, protection for 145 Kms has been protected by artificial means such as Construction of Anti Sea Erosion Bund, Sea Wall, and Beach Nourishment inform Of Geotubes etc. the remaining length is yet to be protected.
Coastal zone monitoring and management is an important task in sustainable development and environmental monitoring. Coast line/ Shoreline is one of the most important linear dynamic features in the earth and its movement due to erosion and deposition is of great Significance in coastal zone management such as development of setback planning, hazard zoning,erosion accretion studies, regional Sediment budgets and modeling of coastal morph dynamics. From the last few year severe loss has been reported in all coastal District of Maharashtra owing ingress of tidal water in urban and rural area, due to climate change, tidal fluctuation, Urbanization etc.
